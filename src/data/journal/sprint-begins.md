# Sprint Begins

96 days. That's what's left before two things happen simultaneously:

- **Jul 4** — board a flight back to Taiwan
- **Jul 5** — sit the JLPT N2 exam

Everything between April 1 and those two dates is the sprint.

## Why document it publicly?

I'm an engineer who spent years in Silicon Valley, a few in Taiwan, and now lands in Singapore. Three cities, three chapters, one life. Most people only see the polished version — the LinkedIn headline, the GitHub profile. This chronicle is the unpolished version: the daily steps, the ramen, the late-night Japanese flashcards.

Engineers who build in public learn faster. The same goes for living in public.

## What I'm tracking

- **Food** — Singapore's hawker culture, Michelin spots, cooking experiments
- **Learning** — JLPT N2 prep, one kanji a day
- **Work** — Building SkyReal, shipping features, talking to users
- **Movement** — runs, cycles, anything that keeps the body working
- **Friends** — intentional time > accidental proximity
- **Adventures** — weekend trips, unexpected detours

## A note on this site

Journal entries come from two places: Notion (synced via CI) and local markdown files like this one. If I'm at a café with no internet, I write here and push later. Engineers should own their own data.
