# What Comes After July

There's something clarifying about having two hard deadlines in the same week.

July 4: flight to Taipei.
July 5: JLPT N2 exam.

After that, the calendar is blank. Not empty in a bad way — blank in the way a whiteboard is blank before a planning session. Full of possibility and slightly terrifying.

## What I know for certain

I'm going back to Taiwan. That part isn't uncertain. It's where my family is, where my roots are, where I want to spend some time before deciding what the next chapter looks like.

Singapore has been a chapter, not a destination. That was always the frame. When I moved here, I told myself: *build something, learn the region, figure out what comes next.* I've done the first two. The third is the one that requires July to pass.

## What I think I know

SkyReal continues regardless of geography. The company doesn't live in a city — it lives in the product and the team. Post-July, I'll probably be running it from Taipei for a while, which will mean a different timezone for some calls but not a fundamental change to how we work.

JLPT N2 — assuming I pass — opens Japan as a real option. Not immediately, not necessarily. But the language barrier being reduced from "significant obstacle" to "manageable friction" changes the calculus on a lot of things.

The next wave of infrastructure I'm interested in building is at the intersection of AI and distributed teams. The region — Southeast Asia, Taiwan, Japan — is where a lot of this is actually being deployed. Being here rather than reading about it from California still seems like the right call.

## What I don't know

I don't know if I'll stay in Asia or head back to the Valley. The honest answer is that it depends on what SkyReal needs, and I don't know what SkyReal will need in September.

I don't know if the JLPT studying has been enough. Probably. Probably not enough. July 5 will tell me.

I don't know what I'll miss about Singapore. Probably more than I expect. It always works that way with places.

## The frame I'm using

I've been thinking of the last several years as a series of experiments rather than a career path. Each one had a question: *Can I build something people want?* *Can I operate across cultures?* *Can I learn a new language as an adult?*

The experiments don't end in July. They just recalibrate.

The 96 days are the sprint before the sprint. The real question — what kind of person do I want to be in the next phase — is the one I'm still working on.

I'll have some answers by July 5. Then I'll start on the next set of questions.
